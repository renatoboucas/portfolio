# AI Positioning

Renato's AI perspective is practical: AI is only useful when it connects to trusted data, real workflows, clear ownership, and maintainable implementation patterns.

He does not position AI as a standalone model-selection exercise. Model providers such as OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini, and Salesforce Agentforce can all be useful, but the right choice depends on the use case, system context, governance needs, integration requirements, and operating model.

Renato treats RAG as knowledge architecture. A RAG project needs trusted source content, document readiness, chunking or content structure, metadata, retrieval relevance, evaluation, permissions, and clear boundaries for what the assistant should and should not answer.

Agentforce can be useful for Salesforce-native workflows, but Renato's AI positioning is broader than Agentforce. His work includes LLM applications, AI assistants, workflow automation, RAG systems, OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini, data engineering, Salesforce Marketing Cloud, Data Cloud, CRM/CDP activation, and marketing technology.

Renato's AI implementation approach usually starts with the business problem, then identifies trusted data and knowledge sources, maps workflow context, designs retrieval or integration patterns, adds human review where needed, and creates a practical implementation roadmap.
